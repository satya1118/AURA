AURA — Autonomous Urban Risk Advisor

Deep-Dive Case Study • Predictive Urban Safety • Satya Devi Varaprasad Gundumogula
Overview
AURA is an AI-driven predictive safety system that forecasts high-risk incidents 30–120 minutes in advance using multimodal data. This deep case study explains the product rationale, technical design, modeling approach, UX patterns, and simulated pilot evaluation.

1. Problem Context

Urban dispatch centers receive thousands of inputs every hour—911/311 calls, CCTV pings, traffic slowdowns, social signals, and weather anomalies. Supervisors must interpret these in real-time, often reactively. This leads to slower emergency responses, congested routes, and suboptimal allocation of field units.

The opportunity: Introduce a predictive layer that processes multimodal signals, ranks emerging hotspots, and recommends resource deployment with explainability.

2. Stakeholders & Needs

StakeholderNeeds
Dispatch Supervisors Prioritized alerts, incident forecasts, explainability, recommended actions
911 Operators Triage consistency, noise reduction, confidence scoring (caller quality)
Patrol Commanders Optimal routing, unit balancing, hotspot projections
City CIO / Ops Compliance, auditability, measurable ROI

3. Operational Pain Points

  • Average urban emergency response latency: ~24 minutes
  • Human recall for high-risk events: ~55%
  • Resource misallocation: 18–22% during peak events
  • Data fragmentation across camera, traffic, and call logs

4. Policy, Privacy & Ethics

  • Demographic fields excluded from model inputs to prevent systemic bias
  • District-level fairness check for false-positive rates
  • Coordinate fuzzing for non-privileged roles (≤100m)
  • Explainability via SHAP + LLM rationale for social context
