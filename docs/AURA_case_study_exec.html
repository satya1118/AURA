AURA — Autonomous Urban Risk Advisor

AI Product Case Study • Predictive Safety & Dispatch Intelligence • Satya Devi Varaprasad Gundumogula
Executive Summary
AURA is a predictive intelligence system designed to forecast urban safety risks 30–120 minutes in advance. By combining multimodal signals—911/311 logs, CCTV anomalies, traffic patterns, weather, event density, and LLM-classified social signals—AURA delivers early-warning alerts, recommended actions, and explainability for dispatch supervisors. The MVP projected a **20–25% reduction in response latency**, a **+24% improvement in dispatch efficiency**, and a potential **$10M annual operational impact** for a large city.

Problem & Opportunity

Dispatch centers are traditionally reactive, operating with fragmented and delayed situational awareness. Supervisors must mentally triage high volumes of unstructured inputs, resulting in slower responses and suboptimal allocation of field units. AURA introduces a predictive layer—providing structured, prioritized intelligence before incidents scale.

Solution Overview (MVP)

  • Lightweight MVP using Python ETL + simulated stream inputs + local batch scoring
  • Designed production architecture for scale (Kafka ingestion, Feast feature store, model registry)
  • Risk forecasting using XGBoost + LLM ensemble with SHAP-based explainability
  • Bias mitigation strategy: demographic inputs excluded; fairness audits at district level
  • Dispatcher dashboard with alert prioritization, rationale, and recommended resourcing

Business Impact

The projected benefits include a **20–25% reduction in response latency**, **+24% improvement in dispatch efficiency**, and a potential **$10M annual operating impact** through improved routing, reduced secondary incidents, and optimized unit scheduling. The MVP validated the feasibility of predictive dispatch intelligence and demonstrated measurable advantages even with lightweight, simulation-based inputs.
